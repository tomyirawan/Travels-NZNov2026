Day 7 · Thursday, 13 November

Te Anau ⇄ Milford Sound

A leisurely Fiordland road-trip day on State Highway 94: alpine valleys, mirror tarns and a boutique afternoon cruise into Piopiotahi — back to a warm dinner in Te Anau.

🚐 Maui River 6-Berth ⛴️ Boutique Small-Boat Cruise 🛏️ Airbnb · 142 Te Anau Terrace 📏 ~240 km round trip ⛽ ~32 L diesel 🌅 Early start · 07:30

⛴️ Boutique Small-Boat vs Big Cruise

Milford Sound cruises share more or less the same route — down the southern side of the fiord, out toward the Tasman Sea, back along the northern wall, stopping at Stirling Falls, Fairy Falls and seal colonies. The real difference is the vessel and the crowd.

👍 Pros — Boutique Small Boat (e.g. Cruise Milford NZ)

  • Capped at 75 passengers (vessel holds 150) — guaranteed half-empty, room to roam.
  • Closer to waterfalls, seal rocks and the Hanging Gardens — the small hull noses in where big boats can’t.
  • The “Glacial Facial” under Stirling Falls feels properly immersive on a small deck.
  • Slightly longer cruise — 1 h 45 min on the water with skipper-led commentary in English only.
  • Higher chance of decent photo angles — no jostling for rail space.
  • Family-owned local operator; commentary is personal, not scripted.

👎 Cons — Boutique Small Boat

  • More expensive than the standard nature cruises (typically NZ$160–180 pp vs ~NZ$130–150).
  • Smaller indoor lounge — if it’s pouring rain and cold, you have less shelter to retreat into.
  • A smaller hull rocks a touch more if the fiord gets choppy near the Tasman Sea entrance.
  • Fewer onboard amenities — coffee/tea/biscuits only, no full café or bar, no buffet lunch option.
  • Fewer departures per day, so less flexibility if you’re running late from the drive.
Best Pick

Boutique small-boat cruise at 14:45 — booked & locked

An early-afternoon departure suits the day well: leaving Te Anau at 07:30 gives you a luxurious 5+ hours to photograph Mirror Lakes and Eglinton Valley in soft morning light, do the Lake Gunn loop, watch kea at Monkey Creek, and still arrive Milford with time for lunch and a foreshore walk. The trade-off vs the 9:45 am sailing is that the terminal is busier mid-afternoon — but on a 75-pax boat that crowd never gets onto your vessel. Heading home by ~5 pm dodges the worst Homer Tunnel queue.

⛽ Fuel Plan — Refuel in Te Anau

There are no fuel stations between Te Anau and Milford Sound (119 km each way). The Maui River runs on a 2.0–2.2L turbo-diesel with a ~70–75 L tank and averages about 13 L/100 km on a manufacturer-stated basis (real-world hilly driving can lift it to ~14 L/100 km).

Best refuel stop — high-flow diesel in Te Anau

🥇 Allied Te Anau (formerly Mobil) — 80 Town Centre, Te Anau
24-hour self-service, generally cheapest diesel in town, easy pull-through bays that comfortably accommodate a 7.2 m motorhome. About 800 m / 2 min from your Airbnb at 142 Te Anau Terrace.

Depart

Roll out of Te Anau on State Highway 94 — the Milford Road

119 km to Milford Sound. Hugs Lake Te Anau for the first 30 km, then climbs into Fiordland National Park. Expect quiet roads — most coach groups from Queenstown won’t reach the same stretch until 09:00–10:00.

07:55
Optional · 10 min

Te Anau Downs jetty

Quick photo stop on the lake — the departure point for Milford Track walkers. Morning is the best chance for calm reflections. Sandflies can be brutal — keep moving if you’re sensitive.

📍 Te Anau Downs Boat Launch

Must · 20 min

Eglinton Valley & Eglinton Flats

First “holy moment” of the drive — broad, glacier-carved valley framed by the Earl Mountains. Morning light + lingering mist around the peaks = best photo conditions of the day. Multiple safe pull-offs along the highway. Lord of the Rings Misty Mountains filming location.

📍 Eglinton Valley
09:05
Must · 25 min

Mirror Lakes boardwalk

400 m return on a flat boardwalk. Morning before the coach groups arrive = best chance for perfect reflections of the Earl Mountains. The famous upside-down sign is at the far end. Roadside parking suits the motorhome — pull in carefully.

📍 Mirror Lakes (Milford Road)
09:45
Optional · 10 min

Knobs Flat — comfort stop

Public toilets, dump station (handy for the motorhome cassette), interpretation displays. Cleanest facilities on the route — useful pause roughly halfway.

📍 Knobs Flat
10:10
Must · 50 min

Lake Gunn Nature Walk

With the early start, you have time for the full 1.4 km loop (~45 min) through moss-draped red beech forest to the quiet lake shore. Wheelchair/pram-friendly. Excellent birdlife — listen for tomtits, robins, bellbirds. Highly photogenic in morning light.

📍 Lake Gunn Nature Walk
11:05
Optional · 10 min

Pop’s View Lookout (Hollyford Valley)

Birds-eye over the forested Hollyford Valley. Only 4–5 parking spots, can be tight for a 7 m motorhome. If full, skip and try again on the return drive.

📍 Pop’s View Lookout
11:25
Must · 15 min

Monkey Creek

Crystal-clear glacial water — refill your bottle straight from the stream. Best chance of the day to see cheeky kea (NZ alpine parrots) and the rare whio (blue duck). Don’t feed the kea — and lock the motorhome (they’ll pry rubber seals off).

📍 Monkey Creek
11:50
Must · 15–25 minTraffic Light

Homer Tunnel — east portal queue

1.27 km single-lane tunnel through solid granite — controlled by traffic lights, up to ~15 min wait. Use the wait to scan the cliffs for kea and avalanche scars (engine off, keep windows up — kea will hop on the bonnet). The tunnel drops at a 1-in-10 gradient on the Milford side; keep it in low gear for engine braking.

📍 Homer Tunnel
12:30
Must · 25 min

The Chasm walk

Easy 400 m return loop on a sealed path. Powerful Cleddau River waterfalls have sculpted basins into the rock — viewing bridges right above them. With the relaxed schedule, definitely worth the stop.

📍 The Chasm Walk
13:05
Arrive

Milford Sound — park & walk to terminal

Paid car park (NZ$10/hour) is ~10 min walk to the terminal; the free car park at Deepwater Basin is ~40 min walk. With ~3.5 h of parking needed and the motorhome, pay for the closer lot — budget NZ$35–40. Card only at payment stations.

📍 Milford Sound Visitor Terminal
13:20
Optional · 20 min

Milford Sound foreshore walk

Bonus from your early arrival: a 10-min stroll along the shoreline gives you the iconic Mitre Peak from land — different perspective from what you’ll see on the boat. Best photo spot of the trip if the cloud cooperates.

13:45

Lunch — terminal café or your own from the motorhome

The Milford Sound Visitor Terminal has a small café with sandwiches, pies, hot drinks. Cheaper and faster: eat the Milford Road Merchant pies you bought this morning at the picnic benches outside, with Mitre Peak as your view.

14:25
Check-in

Cruise Milford NZ counter — 20 min before departure

Second counter on the left inside the terminal. Have your booking confirmation ready (paper or phone screenshot — cell coverage is patchy). Use the restrooms here before boarding.

14:45
Cruise · 1 h 45 minHighlight

Milford Sound Boutique Small-Boat Cruise — fixed departure

Down the southern shore, past Mitre Peak, Stirling Falls (glacial facial!), the Hanging Gardens, seal rocks at Copper Point, then a turn at the Tasman Sea and back along the northern wall past Fairy Falls and Bowen Falls. Complimentary tea, coffee & biscuits onboard. Bring a wind/waterproof jacket — most of the experience is on the open deck.

💡 Practical Tips

What to pack for the cruise

  • Warm layers + windproof jacket — even in November, the deck wind off the fiord is icy.
  • Waterproof shell — Milford gets ~7 m of rain a year; the falls spray you regardless.
  • Sunglasses, sunscreen, hat — the gap between squalls is intense.
  • Camera + phone in a dry bag or zip-lock. Lens cloth.
  • Refillable bottle (top up at Monkey Creek).

Driving the motorhome

  • No cell coverage past Te Anau Downs — pre-download Google Maps offline.
  • Maui River is 3.4 m tall, 7.2 m long, 2.4 m wide — fine through Homer Tunnel (no height issue), but be patient on tight pull-offs.
  • Open-road limit on SH94 is 100 km/h but realistic average is 70–80; let faster cars pass at safe pull-offs.
  • Keep it in a lower gear descending from Homer Tunnel west — engine braking saves your brakes on the 1:10 grade.
  • Heading back, leave Milford by 5 pm to avoid the daily Homer Tunnel queue spike.
