to Christchurch

A final morning at sea with the sperm whales of the Kaikōura Canyon, then south through the wild coast to retire the motorhome and rest under a city sky.

Distance ~183 km

Read this first — timing risks

Verify your booking time

Whale Watch Kaikōura lists 7:45 AM as a departure; check-in opens 30 min prior (7:15 AM) and closes 5 min before departure — no late entries. Cross-check your confirmation email.

Tour can be cancelled by weather

Operators have an 80% refund policy if a whale isn't sighted, and full refund if cancelled for sea conditions. Have a backup plan (e.g. Wings Over Whales scenic flight, or Seal Colony walk).

Return-day rules (Maui)

You have the Drop & Go pack — waste/grey water/toilet cassette and cleaning are covered. You still need to return with full diesel and full LPG bottle with a dated receipt (within 24 hrs). Verify your specific pack inclusions on your rental agreement.

The schedule is comfortable now

Tour returns ~11:00 AM → ~2h 30m drive → 20 min for fuel/LPG = arrival at depot ~2:30 PM. With Drop & Go covering the waste dump, you have a comfortable ~1 hr buffer before the 3:30 PM return.

Headline event

Departure — Catamaran to the Kaikōura Canyon

The reason you flew across the world. From the VIP Captain's Cabin you sit alongside the skipper as hydrophones listen for sperm whale clicks in the 2-km deep trench just offshore. Expect 1–3 whale dives, dusky dolphin pods (often in the hundreds), NZ fur seals, and wandering albatross. Total experience including transfers ≈ 3h 15m. 80% refund if no whale is sighted.

Point Kean Seal Colony

A free, walk-up colony at the tip of the Kaikōura Peninsula. Dozens of NZ fur seals lounge on the rocks, often within a few metres. Mind the 10 m distance rule and never get between a seal and the sea. Skip this if the boat ride ran long — you've already seen plenty of seals from the water.

In transit · SH1 south

Kaikōura coastal highway — scenic but don't linger

SH1 hugs the coast for the first 40 km — railway on one side, surf on the other. The road was rebuilt after the 2016 earthquake. Tempting to stop everywhere, but the clock is ticking. Pick at most one short pull-over below.

Don't forget — Road User Charges

Diesel motorhomes in NZ pay RUC separately from pump diesel — Maui passes this through at NZD $8.04 per 100 km for 4–6 berths, billed at hire end based on the odometer reading. For ~183 km today that's roughly $14.70, on top of pump diesel. Across your full 13-day trip this adds up to a substantial line item — make sure your final invoice reflects it.
