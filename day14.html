i · The shape of the dayThree stages

This is not a one-flight day. It unfolds in three honest windows — each with a different character and a different gift.

Stage 01 · Pre-dawn
Christchurch
02:30 – 06:20 NZDT
Wake in the dark. Quiet airport. The first leg out of New Zealand. If you grab a right-hand window, the Southern Alps at sunrise — your final South Island lookout.
Stage 02 · Mid-morning
Brisbane
07:05 – 10:35 AEST
A real layover. Three and a half hours airside. A proper breakfast, a long walk, decompression. The halfway point. Australia for an hour or two.
Stage 03 · Late afternoon
Singapore
16:30 SGT onward
Home soil. A real evening ahead. Tropical air on the face. The first bowl of laksa or chicken rice. Six travellers, returned.

ii · Verify before travelThings to confirm

Before you board

Is this one connecting ticket or two? If one PNR, your bags are tagged through CHC → SIN and you stay airside in Brisbane. If two separate tickets, you must clear Australian immigration in Brisbane, collect bags, and re-check in — adding 60–90 minutes and possibly requiring a transit visa or ETA.

Australia transit: Singapore passport holders qualify for the free Electronic Travel Authority (ETA). Other passports in the group should verify their own requirements.

Baggage allowance on Qantas international economy is typically 30 kg + 7 kg cabin — confirm against your fare class.

Right-side window seat on QF132 for sunrise over the Alps. Check seat selection in advance.

iii · Hour by hourThe full day

19 Nov · 18:00 NZDT
Final group dinner in Christchurch Must 90 min

The real closure meal of the trip. Three good options near the CBD:

  • Riverside Market — casual food hall, easy for 6 with mixed cravings.
  • OGB Café & Bar — historic Edwardian building, suitable for a proper toast.
  • Twenty Seven Steps, New Regent Street — modern NZ cuisine. Booking essential.

At the table: one round of "favourite moment", settle shared expenses, confirm passports.

19 Nov · 20:00 NZDT
Pack, weigh, sleep early Must 90 min + sleep
  • Manuka honey / wine → checked bag, wrapped.
  • Power banks → cabin only.
  • Boots in plastic bags. Mud off where possible.
  • Singapore Arrival Card filled out online tonight.
  • Lights out by 21:30. Five hours of sleep ahead.
20 Nov · 02:30 NZDT
Wake · final check · depart 35 min

Quiet, no breakfast (eat on QF132 or in Brisbane). Taxi pre-booked the night before. Depart 02:45.

20 Nov · 03:05 NZDT
Arrive Christchurch International Must

Terminal opens 03:00. You'll be among the first in — peaceful, quiet, no queues. Check-in, bag drop, passport, biosecurity, security. ~90 min for the lot.

20 Nov · 04:45 NZDT
Last NZ coffee Optional 60 min

Cafés airside open early. A flat white in the departure lounge is the most fitting goodbye drink this country offers. Resist new luggage.

20 Nov · 06:20 NZDT
QF132 · Wheels up Departure

Right-side window, if you can — for the first 30–60 minutes, sunrise over the Southern Alps is your final South Island lookout. Same mountains. Different view. Same God who held you in them.

20 Nov · 07:05 AEST
Touchdown · Brisbane Terminal I Layover

If one connecting ticket: stay airside, head to International Transit. Bags continue to SIN. If two tickets: clear Australian immigration, claim bags, re-check.

20 Nov · 08:00 AEST
Brisbane airside breakfast Must 90 min

You skipped breakfast in Christchurch — eat properly now. Brisbane T-I has a good range of cafés (The Coffee Club, Plonk, Vintaged Bar + Grill) plus fast options. Verify current outlets on arrival. This is the real "meal stop" of the day.

20 Nov · 09:30 AEST
Walk, stretch, freshen up Optional 60 min

Brisbane T-I is long — a 20-minute walk loosens up jet-lagged legs (medically valuable, not just nice). Or pay into Plaza Premium Lounge (~A$65/person) for a shower and quiet sit-down. One last group photo at the gate — you're now exactly halfway home.

20 Nov · 10:35 AEST
QF51 · Wheels up Departure

The long leg. ~8 hours westward into evening. Sleep the first half. Write memories down. Share earbuds. Don't open the laptop — email can wait. This is the last quiet of the trip.

20 Nov · 16:30 SGT
Touchdown · Singapore Changi Terminal 1 Home

Tropical air on the face. SG residents through automated lanes. Declare food honestly (sealed honey fine; fresh fruit and muddy boots are not). For 6 with bags, two taxis or a pre-booked Maxi Cab beat MRT every time. ~60 min from gate to home.

20 Nov · 19:30 SGT
Homecoming dinner Optional

The afternoon arrival gives you a real evening. Three honest choices:

  • Hawker centre — Old Airport Road, East Coast Lagoon, Newton, or Tiong Bahru. After 14 days of pies and motorhome cooking, the first bowl of laksa is the proper homecoming.
  • Jewel Changi sit-down — eat before dispersing. Higher energy required.
  • Everyone goes home. The group chat lives on. This is no failure of friendship after fourteen days and a sixteen-hour journey.

iv · Memory laneWhat we will keep

Two weeks ago we were strangers to this land — and now the land has become a roll-call of names we say like prayers: Tekapo, Pukaki, Wanaka, Milford, Moeraki, Akaroa. We arrived counting the days. We leave counting the moments.

We remember the first sight of Aoraki — larger than any photograph promised. The lupins along Tekapo's edge, purple and pink as if the alpine grass itself had blushed. The Hooker Valley swing bridges swaying under six pairs of boots. The Tasman terminal lake holding icebergs like patient sculptures. The road to Milford that seemed to fold open another wonder every ten minutes, as though the country were generous to the point of showing off.

We remember smaller things too. The motorhome's hum at dusk. The grocery runs at New World. The shared cooking, the shared washing-up, the small negotiations over the last slice of toast. Cold mornings when someone made the first coffee without being asked. Roadside pee-stops on tussock plains. The Milky Way over Lake Pukaki, brighter than we knew the sky could be. Sheep, sheep, more sheep. The Moeraki boulders sitting like stones that had been waiting longer than any of us will ever live.

And we remember the laughter — that, more than anything, is what we will keep. The bad puns. The singalongs. The inside jokes no one outside this six will fully understand. The driver swaps. The "five more minutes" that became thirty. The photos retaken because someone blinked, and the better photos that came from not retaking them.

The South Island gave us mountains. We gave each other something quieter and harder to name — a friendship deepened by a thousand small choices to be patient, to be cheerful, to be present. That is the souvenir worth more than any.

vi · To the six of usCongratulations & thanks

You showed up early on cold mornings. You stayed cheerful through long driving days. You shared the cooking and the cleaning without keeping score. You laughed at the same jokes until they got funnier by repetition. You looked out for the slowest hiker. You waited at the trailhead. You drove when others were tired. You navigated when others were lost. You took the photo even when you wanted to be in it.

Fourteen days of close quarters is a real test, and the six of you passed it with grace. Different temperaments, different paces, different favourite songs — and not one fight that mattered. That is rarer than people realise, and it is the truest measure of this trip.

Thank you — for the patience, for the positive attitude, for the willingness to be tired and still kind. Thank you for the way each of you brought something the others didn't have. Thank you for trusting the plan, trusting each other, and trusting the road.

The South Island was the setting. The six of you were the story.

Welcome home.
