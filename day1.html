Getting around · The Bus Brief

Route 29 is your friend today — the direct airport↔city run via Fendalton. Saturday frequency is roughly every 30 minutes, last bus back from the city around 22:32.

  • FareOne-way $4.20 adult. Tap on with contactless card/phone (Apple Pay, Google Pay) at the Motu Move reader on the right when boarding.
  • Boarding · AirportExit Door 9 — northern end of International Arrivals, next to the Novotel hotel. Undercover walk all the way.
  • Boarding · LyLo sideStop "Memorial Ave near Spitfire Square" — about a 5-min walk from LyLo.
  • City terminusBus Interchange, Platform C — corner of Lichfield & Colombo. Award-winning, clean, with free toilets.
  • Free transfersApply within 2 hours on the same card — useful if you split off.

Must · Lookout

Cathedral Square

Cathedral Square, Christchurch Central

The geographic and emotional heart of the city. See the still-being-rebuilt ChristChurch Cathedral (heavily damaged in the 2011 quake, slowly returning), the silver Chalice sculpture (commemorating the new millennium), and the open plaza where post-quake life is reclaiming itself. Five minutes here can tell you everything about why Christchurch matters.

Tūranga Central Library — 5th-floor terrace

60 Cathedral Square · Free entry, open Sat till 17:00

Heads up — on Saturdays Tūranga closes at 5 pm, so if you want this one, swing here before Cathedral Square (swap the order). Take the lift to Level 5 for a free rooftop terrace with the city's best central-CBD view, looking straight back at the cathedral construction. Bean bags, plants, fresh wind, and a brilliantly designed civic space.

Must · Shop & photo

New Regent Street

New Regent Street, Christchurch Central

A single pedestrianised block of pastel Spanish-Mission façades — Christchurch's prettiest street. The heritage tram clinks through every few minutes. Browse the gelato shop (Rollickin' Gelato), boutique craft stores, Kiwi-made jewellery, and Maori art studios. Easily the group's best photo stop of the day; golden-hour light should be perfect.

Must · Architecture

Cardboard (Transitional) Cathedral

234 Hereford Street · Closes 12:30 pm Sat — admire exterior

Designed by Pritzker Prize-winning Japanese architect Shigeru Ban after the 2011 earthquake. Made of 96 huge cardboard tubes, glass, and steel — yet engineered to last 50+ years. On Saturday afternoons the interior is closed, but the A-frame profile, stained-glass entrance, and the surrounding Memorial Wall are striking from outside, especially as the light softens. A 10-min walk east from New Regent Street.

Must · View point

Bridge of Remembrance & Avon River

Cashel Street, over the Avon

WWI memorial arch spanning the Ōtākaro/Avon River — restored after the quake and the city's most photogenic landmark at dusk. Walk along the river path (5–10 mins) for views of weeping willows, punt boats gliding past, and reflections of the arch on the water. The group can split here — some stay on the riverside for photos, others wander into the Botanic Gardens just across.

Optional · Light hike

Christchurch Botanic Gardens

Rolleston Avenue · Open daily until 20:30

21 hectares of green calm bordered by the Avon. In early November the Rose Garden is just bursting into bloom (250+ varieties), and the magnolias and rhododendrons are spectacular. Pick the short loop: Bridge of Remembrance → Peacock Fountain → Rose Garden → Cuningham House conservatory → out at the Armagh St gate. About a 30-min easy walk on flat paths — the perfect "stretch the plane legs" warm-up for the road trip. Soft evening light is gorgeous.

Dinner · Must

Riverside Market

96 Oxford Terrace · Open Sat till 21:00

Christchurch's beating heart for food — 60+ stalls and bars across two levels, every cuisine you can think of (Vietnamese, Mexican, Italian, Japanese, Indian, Greek, Cantonese, vegan, oysters, gelato). Perfect for a group of 6 with mixed tastes: everyone orders separately, then meets at one of the long shared tables to swap bites. Have a craft beer or local Canterbury wine. Highlights: Smokey T's (BBQ), Hikari (sushi train), Buon Appetito (Italian), Rollickin' (gelato dessert). Budget ~NZ$25–35 per person.

Tips for the six of you

Small stuff that matters

Weather, early Nov

Spring shoulder season — average 8°C to 18°C, often windy and changeable. Pack a light rain shell, layers, and sunglasses. Sun is strong even when cool.

Money / payments

NZ is largely cashless. Contactless cards work for buses. Have a small amount of NZD for tips, markets, tram. Riverside Market vendors all take card.

Group logistics

Six is a sweet spot. Agree on a meeting point and time at each transition (Cathedral Square / Riverside / Bus Interchange) so anyone who lingers can rejoin easily. Share a WhatsApp pin.

Daylight, 7 Nov

Sunset around 8:20 pm, civil twilight to 8:55 pm. Golden hour lands beautifully on the Bridge of Remembrance and Botanic Gardens — schedule those for the back half.
