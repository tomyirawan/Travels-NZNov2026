⛽ Strategy Note

Frankton roundabout · skip Queenstown CBD

SH6 / SH6A junction at Lake Hayes

At Frankton (just east of Queenstown), stay on SH6 toward Cromwell — do not turn left into Queenstown town centre. This keeps you on the Cromwell route and naturally avoids the Crown Range Road altogether. Queenstown CBD traffic with a 7.25 m motorhome is best skipped.

Frankton diesel is more expensive than Cromwell or Wānaka. Push through if your tank is still half-full.

🚫 No detour into QT → Continue SH6 east

Webb's Fruit

25 Luggate–Cromwell Road · 7 km north of Cromwell, on SH6

Family-run Central Otago orchard shop right on the SH6 route — cherries, apricots, peaches, nectarines and apples direct from the grower. November is early cherry season, so expect the first crop. Honesty-box self-service when staff aren't in; toilets on site. Rated 4.6★, locally loved.

Recommended fuel plan

Option A · Cheapest: Leave Te Anau on whatever you have above quarter-tank, push through to Z Cromwell at ~15:30 for ~$2.15/L. Top up to half. Tomorrow morning, fill fully at McKeown Wānaka 24/7 at ~$1.90/L before leaving — saves you NZ$15–25 vs Queenstown/Te Anau pump prices on a 60 L fill.

Option B · Nearest / safest: Top up to full at Caltex Te Anau (08:00, ~$2.30/L). 56 L gets you the whole day with a comfortable buffer. Top up tomorrow morning at McKeown Wānaka before continuing.

Driving the Maui River

What to know before you go.

01

Crown Range avoided — by design

By following SH94 → SH97 → SH6 via Cromwell, you skip the Crown Range Road entirely. No hairpins, no 1,076 m alpine pass, no anxious 7.25 m-motorhome moments. The trade-off is ~50 km more driving on much easier roads.

02

Devil's Staircase warning

SH6 along the southeastern shore of Lake Wakatipu is winding and narrow in places. Drop into 3rd or 4th gear, use engine braking on descents, and pull over at signposted "slow vehicle bays" — locals drive this road fast.

03

Dump station tonight

Hampshire Holiday Parks Wānaka has an on-site dump station. Empty the toilet cassette and grey-water tank tonight; you've gone 2 days since the last empty. Refill fresh water before bed.

04

Plug in for 240V

Two days of driving means the house battery is well below full. The solar trickle isn't enough — book a powered site and plug in tonight for a full 14–16 h charge. You'll want everything topped up before tomorrow.

05

That Wānaka Tree timing

Sunset on 14 Nov is approximately 8:45 pm in Wānaka. Sunrise photographers gather from 5:30 am — but for most, the warm sunset light from the western side of the willow is the magic shot. Check water levels: if the lake's low, you may need wet feet.

06

The motorhome plays well

The Maui River's reverse camera, automatic transmission and Mercedes Sprinter chassis make this drive a non-event compared to the Crown Range. Plan ~5h 25min driving time + your stops = 8:00 am start, 6:00 pm arrival is realistic.
