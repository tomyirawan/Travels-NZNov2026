Same loop, sharper rhythm. Hydro Attack departs from the Queenstown waterfront — a 2-minute walk from Hampshire — so the adrenaline hit happens with zero driving detour. That recovers a clean 90 minutes versus the Shotover Jet option, and lets Arrowtown and Glenorchy both breathe.

Why Arrowtown morning, Glenorchy afternoon?

The day is built around two natural rhythms: a quiet old town, and a long lakeshore drive. Both have a "right" time of day, and they sit on opposite sides of Queenstown — so the loop is clean if you sequence them correctly.

Light & crowds favour morning

Buckingham Street's sycamores and miners' cottages face east-southeast — soft, low light hits the verandahs before 10:30. The first Queenstown coach tours arrive 10:30–11:30 and motorhome parking on Ramshaw Lane tightens fast. Arrive 09:00, leave by 11:30.

Glenorchy Road is a westbound drive

From Queenstown, the road curves northwest along the lake's western shore. Going out in the afternoon puts the sun behind your right shoulder — every lookout (especially Bennetts Bluff) photographs cleanly, and the red Glenorchy boat shed glows against the Humboldts from 16:00 onward.

Hydro Attack on the lakefront, not a canyon drive

Swapping Shotover Jet for Hydro Attack saves the round-trip to Arthurs Point — ~75 minutes recovered. That extra time goes straight into Glenorchy's golden hour: you'll reach Bennetts Bluff in good light and have time for the full Glenorchy Walkway loop. Same adrenaline category, far better timing.

WET / DRY VERDICT

Hydro Attack is the dry-clothes option

Sealed acrylic cockpit, life jacket only, you step in and step out wearing what you arrived in. Critical because the Glenorchy drive comes immediately after — nobody wants to spend two hours in a motorhome in damp jeans. With Shotover Jet, even with the supplied waterproof coats, your hair and the cuffs of your trousers usually come off the boat damp; allow 15 minutes at base to towel off and warm up before driving. If staying dry matters → Hydro Attack.

PHYSICAL IMPACT VERDICT

Different categories of intensity

Both have the same contraindications (pregnancy, serious heart conditions, back/neck injury), but the type of force is different. Shotover Jet is sustained lateral G — like a hard-driven sports car for 25 minutes; passengers brace shoulder-to-shoulder. Hydro Attack is intermittent stunt-plane G — vertical leaps and underwater dives, alone in a 1-person cockpit. Motion-sickness reports are higher for Hydro Attack (the operator explicitly recommends no big meal within 2 hours). For nervous first-timers or anyone prone to seasickness → Shotover Jet feels more contained. For thrill-maximisers → Hydro Attack hits harder per minute.

For today's loop · timing, dryness & efficiency winner

Hydro Attack wins by ~75 minutes of recovered day-time, and the recovery comes at the most valuable point — early afternoon, just before the Glenorchy drive. Those minutes go directly into Bob's Cove and golden-hour Glenorchy, the day's headline moments. It also wins on dryness — the sealed cockpit keeps you in your day clothes, so you can step out and into the motorhome for the Glenorchy drive without a towelling-off stop. The trade-offs are real but bounded: a fun-park ride on the lake instead of a canyon ride on the river, and slightly higher motion-sickness risk if the pilot goes wild. For an itinerary that already includes Arrowtown and Glenorchy and a fuel-stop, Hydro Attack is the right tool.

PARK

Park the motorhome — Ramshaw Lane car park

Ramshaw Lane, behind Buckingham St · long-vehicle spaces along the back fence · Google Map ↗

The main Buckingham Street parking is for cars only. Ramshaw Lane is two minutes' walk from the heritage street and the only sensible motorhome park in town. Free, no time limit, public toilets on-site. Get here before 09:30 — by 10:30 the coach tours arrive and the long bays go first.

Heads-up Do NOT try to drive a 7m motorhome down Buckingham St itself. It's one-way, narrow, angled-parking on both sides. Park at Ramshaw and walk in.

WANDER

Buckingham Street — heritage stroll & coffee

Buckingham St, 1860s miners' cottages, 24 trees each side · Google Map ↗

Walk east down Buckingham toward the Arrow River. Sixty-plus original gold-rush buildings still standing, many now boutique shops, galleries, and cafés. The sycamores arching over the street are spectacular in spring green by mid-November. Don't miss the Mary Cotter sycamore outside the old Cotter cottage — there's a small plaque dedicated to a two-year-old in 1867.

Coffee + breakfast at Provisions of Arrowtown (34 Buckingham St) — set in a historic miner's cottage with a back garden. The Eggs Benedict and the pastries are both legendary; NZ$18–32 for a main. Or a quicker stop at The Chop Shop (Arrow Lane) — locals' favourite for flat whites.

Sweet stop for the group The Remarkable Sweet Shop and Patagonia Chocolates are both on Buckingham — pick up roadtrip snacks for the Glenorchy drive.
10:10
30 min
HISTORY

Arrowtown Chinese Settlement — free, riverside

Bush Creek end of Buckingham St · Google Map ↗

A two-minute walk past the western end of Buckingham, beside Bush Creek, you'll find a cluster of restored stone-and-tin huts. From 1869, Chinese gold-miners — invited by the government when European miners moved west to the Coast — were forced to live separately here. Ah Lum's Store is the standout: fully restored 1880s provisions shop with original counters and ledgers. Information panels along the path, free entry, allow 25–30 minutes.

HONEY

The Buzz Stop Honey Centre & Café — Country Lane, Frankton

7 Hansen Rd, Frankton · Country Lane Retail Village · 7 days, cafe 8:30–3pm · Google Map ↗

Nick Cameron's family has been beekeeping in Central Otago for over 100 years. The centre is part shop, part observation hive (thousands of bees behind glass), part café with views of the Remarkables. Taste the full Active Manuka range from 5+ to 24+, plus native honeys you won't find at the Cromwell honey shed — rātā, kāmahi, southern thyme. Pick up jars for home and the famously good honey-glazed liege waffles in the café.

If you've got energy for one more activity, the Spin Your Own Honey hands-on tour runs at 10:00 and 15:00 — too tight for today's plan. Buy a jar instead and keep moving toward lunch and the Hydro Attack shark on the lake.

Why the second honey stop is worth it The Cromwell honesty-box shed yesterday was farm-direct single-source; Buzzstop is a curated NZ-wide tasting bench. Different products, different role. Some travellers stock the van here for the rest of the road trip.

SHARK · ADRENALINE

Hydro Attack — Lapsley Butson Wharf, Queenstown lakefront

Earnslaw Park, Beach St · NZ$179 pp · ages 6+, ≤195cm, ≤105kg · Google Map ↗

Drive the motorhome from Frankton back to Queenstown (12 min) and park at Boundary St car park or right at Hampshire (free, 2 min walk to the wharf). Check-in at the kiosk 10 minutes before your slot — Hydro Attack runs regular departures 09:10 to 17:10. Pre-book online to lock the time.

Each ride is 1-on-1 with the pilot in the Seabreacher X cockpit — 15 minutes of high-speed lake action: 80 km/h on the surface, 40 km/h underwater dives, 90° rolls, doughnut spins, vertical leaps where the entire 6m shark launches out of the water. The pilot dials it from mild to wild based on your call. For a group of 6, the rides run back-to-back from a single check-in — you'll be done inside 75 minutes from arrival.

How the group rotates One person rides, the rest watch from the wharf (rides happen offshore but you can see the dives and leaps clearly) or grab coffee at Perky's Floating Bar or any Beach St café 30 seconds away. Total clock-time impact: ~75 minutes regardless of group size.
Weather caveat Hydro Attack is more weather-sensitive than Shotover Jet — Lake Wakatipu gets choppy on windy days. Call 0508 HYDRO ATTACK at least 1 hour before your slot to confirm. If cancelled, they offer a boat-trip-and-shark spectator option or a full refund with 24+ hr notice.

FUEL

BP Queenstown Truckstop — high-flow diesel

131 Gorge Rd, Queenstown · 24/7 · truckstop pricing · Google Map ↗

4 minutes from Hampshire. The only true truckstop in Queenstown — high-flow diesel nozzles, drive-through bays sized for tour buses, and prices typically 15–25¢/L cheaper than the BP Connect or Z on Frankton Rd. There's no fuel between Queenstown and Glenorchy, and you don't want to drive that road on a quarter tank. Top up to full (~40 L for the Maui River — see fuel panel below).

Why here, not on Frankton Road BP Connect Queenstown (1094 Frankton Rd) and Z Queenstown are both forecourt-only, standard nozzles, retail pricing — fine for cars, slow for a Maui's 95-litre tank. Gorge Rd Truckstop fills it in under 4 minutes.

SHORT WALK

Bob's Cove Track — forest, jetty, lookout

Glenorchy-Queenstown Rd, 17 km from Queenstown · large gravel car park · Google Map ↗

A 1.5 km loop through beech forest opens to a turquoise cove, then a short steep climb to a lookout knoll. Roughly 30 min walking + 15 min at the jetty + 10 min at the lookout — with the Hydro Attack version of today's plan you get the full hour here instead of a rushed half-loop. This is one of the best short walks in the whole Wakatipu basin — the sheltered cove looks tropical on a calm afternoon (lake's still glacier-cold; nobody's swimming in November). Toilets at the car park, easy motorhome parking.

Bennetts Bluff Lookout — the premier viewpoint

Glenorchy-Queenstown Rd, ~25 km from Queenstown · purpose-built 2021 lookout · Google Map ↗

This is THE photo of the Glenorchy drive. Two hundred metres past the old roadside pull-out, a proper purpose-built car park (large enough for the Maui), a 3-minute walk to the platform, picnic tables, toilets. PHOTOS + WALK

Glenorchy Wharf, Red Shed & Walkway — golden hour

Mull St / Oban St · free public car park at the wharf · Google Map ↗

Park at the lakefront. The famous red boat shed sits on the jetty — once a freight depot for the TSS Earnslaw, now the most photographed shed in the South Island. The Humboldt Mountains rise behind it; in afternoon light (~17:30 in mid-November) the peaks are still in sun while the shed sits in shadow — perfect for a silhouette shot.

Then the Glenorchy Walkway — a flat, easy 1-hour loop on a boardwalk through the lagoon wetland behind town. Bird life (paradise ducks, scaup, the occasional fernbird), 360° mountain views, almost no tourists. The Mt Earnslaw glacier glows pink at sunset. This is the moment you remember the trip for. With the Hydro Attack plan you have the full 90 minutes here — enough for the walkway AND a slow coffee on the waterfront before dinner.

Lord of the Rings note If you're a fan: the flats north of Glenorchy (toward Paradise) appeared as Lothlórien, Isengard, and the muster of Rohan. Paradise itself is 20 km further on a partly-gravel road — too far for today, but the walkway crosses some of the same vista.

QUEENSTOWN HILL

How to fit Queenstown Hill in

Trailhead at top of Belfast Terrace · 5.6 km return, 430 m climb · Google Map ↗

The Queenstown Hill "Time Walk" to the Basket of Dreams sculpture is a 2–3 hour return hike with 360° views of Lake Wakatipu, the Remarkables and Cecil Peak. It earns inclusion on a Queenstown day — and the Hydro Attack version of this plan gives you slightly more room to consider it.

Two ways to swap it in:

Option A — sunrise hike (best fit): Start at 06:00 from Hampshire (10-min walk to Belfast Terrace trailhead). Summit by 07:15, back down by 08:30. Then proceed with the day as planned — Arrowtown departure shifts to 09:15. Best views, no crowds, parking guaranteed, no impact on Hydro Attack or Glenorchy.

Option B — swap Hydro Attack: Skip the shark ride, do the hike instead starting 12:30. Summit by 13:45, back to van by 15:00. This saves NZ$179pp and gets you a much bigger physical adventure, but you lose the "wow" moment of the adrenaline activity and lose the quirky group photo. Fuel can still slot in at 15:00.

Don't try to do all three on the same day Arrowtown + Glenorchy + Hydro Attack + Queenstown Hill on one day will feel rushed and you'll lose the slow Glenorchy golden-hour moment, which is the day's headline. Sunrise hike (Option A) is the only clean way to fit all four.

The Maui River 6-berth on a 5.0L Mercedes Sprinter chassis burns roughly 13–17 L/100km depending on hills and load — the Glenorchy road has gentle gradients, so today comes in around 15 L/100km. Distance breakdown: Hampshire → Arrowtown (22 km) → Frankton (22 km) → Queenstown lakefront for Hydro Attack (5 km) → Gorge Rd Truckstop (3 km) → Glenorchy (47 km) → Hampshire (47 km). Total ~146 km, ~22 L for the day — a few km extra over the Shotover-Jet version because the activity is in town, but no different in practice.

Recommended refuel: BP Queenstown Truckstop, 131 Gorge Road — Queenstown's only true high-flow diesel truckstop, open 24/7, with drive-through bays and truckstop pricing (typically 15–25¢/L below the Frankton Road BP Connect and Z stations). It sits 4 minutes from Hampshire and 6 minutes from the Glenorchy road exit, so it slots in perfectly between the morning Arrowtown loop and the afternoon Glenorchy drive. There is no fuel between Queenstown and Glenorchy — top up to full before heading west.

Backup options if Gorge Rd is closed: BP Connect Queenstown (1094 Frankton Rd) and NPD Frankton both have diesel — forecourt-only, retail pricing, but reliable. NPD usually undercuts BP/Z by a few cents.
