Day 05 Wed · 11 Nov 2026 Queenstown · Arrowtown · Glenorchy

Gold-rush cottages,
then a road into Paradise.

A circular day from Hampshire Lakeview — sycamores and stonemasons in the cool of an Arrowtown morning, then a shark-shaped Seabreacher diving and leaping on Lake Wakatipu, then the long curve west along the lake's edge to Glenorchy, where the road runs out at the foot of the Humboldt Mountains.

distance~138 km
diesel~21 L
sunset~20:30
activityHydro Attack

Why Arrowtown morning, Glenorchy afternoon?

The day is built around two natural rhythms: a quiet old town, and a long lakeshore drive. Both have a "right" time of day, and they sit on opposite sides of Queenstown — so the loop is clean if you sequence them correctly.

Light & crowds favour morning

Buckingham Street's sycamores and miners' cottages face east-southeast — soft, low light hits the verandahs before 10:30. The first Queenstown coach tours arrive 10:30–11:30 and motorhome parking on Ramshaw Lane tightens fast. Arrive 09:00, leave by 11:30.

Glenorchy Road is a westbound drive

From Queenstown, the road curves northwest along the lake's western shore. Going out in the afternoon puts the sun behind your right shoulder — every lookout (especially Bennetts Bluff) photographs cleanly, and the red Glenorchy boat shed glows against the Humboldts from 16:00 onward.

The loop has only one unavoidable backtrack

Hampshire → Arrowtown → Frankton (Buzzstop) → Queenstown lakefront (Hydro Attack) → fuel → Glenorchy → Hampshire. There is no road around Lake Wakatipu's head — the return from Glenorchy retraces the same highway, but in evening light it's a different drive entirely.

Out past Frankton, over the Shotover Bridge, with the Remarkables on your right and Coronet Peak rising ahead. Lake Hayes appears on the left — if it's a stillwater morning, the lake doubles every mountain. Worth pulling into the Lake Hayes Pavilion car park for a five-minute reflection shot.

PARK

Park the motorhome — Ramshaw Lane car park

Ramshaw Lane, behind Buckingham St · long-vehicle spaces along the back fence · Google Map ↗

The main Buckingham Street parking is for cars only. Ramshaw Lane is two minutes' walk from the heritage street and the only sensible motorhome park in town. Free, no time limit, public toilets on-site. Get here before 09:30 — by 10:30 the coach tours arrive and the long bays go first.

Heads-up Do NOT try to drive a 7m motorhome down Buckingham St itself. It's one-way, narrow, angled-parking on both sides. Park at Ramshaw and walk in.
09:10
60 min
WANDER

Buckingham Street — heritage stroll & coffee

Buckingham St, 1860s miners' cottages, 24 trees each side · Google Map ↗

Walk east down Buckingham toward the Arrow River. Sixty-plus original gold-rush buildings still standing, many now boutique shops, galleries, and cafés. The sycamores arching over the street are spectacular in spring green by mid-November. Don't miss the Mary Cotter sycamore outside the old Cotter cottage — there's a small plaque dedicated to a two-year-old in 1867.

Coffee + breakfast at Provisions of Arrowtown (34 Buckingham St) — set in a historic miner's cottage with a back garden. The Eggs Benedict and the pastries are both legendary; NZ$18–32 for a main. Or a quicker stop at The Chop Shop (Arrow Lane) — locals' favourite for flat whites.

Sweet stop for the group The Remarkable Sweet Shop and Patagonia Chocolates are both on Buckingham — pick up roadtrip snacks for the Glenorchy drive.
10:10
30 min
HISTORY

Arrowtown Chinese Settlement — free, riverside

Bush Creek end of Buckingham St · Google Map ↗

A two-minute walk past the western end of Buckingham, beside Bush Creek, you'll find a cluster of restored stone-and-tin huts. From 1869, Chinese gold-miners — invited by the government when European miners moved west to the Coast — were forced to live separately here. Ah Lum's Store is the standout: fully restored 1880s provisions shop with original counters and ledgers. Information panels along the path, free entry, allow 25–30 minutes.

10:40
45 min
OPTIONAL · MUSEUM

Lakes District Museum + gold panning — if it's drizzling

49 Buckingham St · 9am–5pm daily · NZ$12 adult · Google Map ↗

Called one of NZ's best small museums — basement set out as a recreated Arrowtown streetscape with the original bank, blacksmith, and 1875 bakery oven. They also hire gold pans (NZ$5) so you can wade into the Arrow River out the back and actually find flakes. Skip this if the weather is clear and you want more outdoor time at Glenorchy.

Back over the Shotover Bridge, toward Frankton. The next stops cluster within a few minutes of each other — Buzzstop honey, lunch in Frankton, then a short drive back to the Queenstown lakefront for Hydro Attack.

PART 02 · MIDDAY

Bees, honey & (optional) canyons

11:55
45 min
HONEY

The Buzz Stop Honey Centre & Café — Country Lane, Frankton

7 Hansen Rd, Frankton · Country Lane Retail Village · 7 days, cafe 8:30–3pm · Google Map ↗

Nick Cameron's family has been beekeeping in Central Otago for over 100 years. The centre is part shop, part observation hive (thousands of bees behind glass), part café with views of the Remarkables. Taste the full Active Manuka range from 5+ to 24+, plus native honeys you won't find at the Cromwell honey shed — rātā, kāmahi, southern thyme. Pick up jars for home and the famously good honey-glazed liege waffles in the café.

If you've got energy for one more activity, the Spin Your Own Honey hands-on tour runs at 10:00 and 15:00 — too tight for today's plan. Buy a jar instead and keep moving toward lunch and the lakefront.

Why the second honey stop is worth it The Cromwell honesty-box shed yesterday was farm-direct single-source; Buzzstop is a curated NZ-wide tasting bench. Different products, different role. Some travellers stock the van here for the rest of the road trip.

40 min
QUICK LUNCH

Lunch in Frankton — eat & go

Choose by what suits the group · Area map ↗

Light and fast — Hydro Attack on a full stomach is regrettable (the operator explicitly recommends no large meal within 2 hours of the ride due to g-forces). The day ends with a proper dinner in Glenorchy anyway. Best options:

Quick & light: Boat Shed Café on Marine Parade — lake views, cabinet food, NZ$15–22 mains, plenty of motorhome parking. Quick service.

Sit-down + restock: Five Mile mall — supermarket-cluster lunch (sushi, dumplings, salads) plus the Pak'nSave next door to restock the van fridge before the Glenorchy run.

Alternative: van lunch by the lake Drive 3 min to Frankton Beach (Hansen Rd end) — gravel beach, public picnic tables, view of the Remarkables across the bay. Reheat last night's dinner + a Buzzstop manuka drizzle. Free, scenic, fastest of all.
13:30
75 min (group of 6)
SHARK · ADRENALINE

Hydro Attack — Lapsley Butson Wharf, Queenstown lakefront

Earnslaw Park, Beach St (kiosk in front of Patagonia Chocolates) · NZ$179 pp · ages 6+, ≤195cm, ≤105kg · Google Map ↗

Drive Frankton → Queenstown (12 min) and park at Hampshire (free, your existing pitch — 2 min walk to the wharf) or Boundary St car park if Hampshire is awkward to back into. Check-in at the lakefront kiosk 10 minutes before your slot — Hydro Attack runs regular departures 09:10 to 17:10. Pre-book online to lock the time.

Each ride is 1-on-1 with the pilot in the Seabreacher X cockpit — 15 minutes of high-speed lake action: 80 km/h on the surface, 40 km/h underwater dives, 90° rolls, doughnut spins, and vertical leaps where the entire shark launches out of the water. The pilot dials it from mild to wild based on your call. For a group of 6, the rides run back-to-back from a single check-in — total clock-time impact is ~75 minutes from arrival.

How the group rotates One person rides, the rest watch from the wharf (rides happen offshore but you can see the leaps and dives clearly) or grab coffee on Beach St 30 seconds away. The 1-on-1 format means no waiting around for slow boarders — sequence runs efficiently.
What to wear, how wet you get The pneumatic-sealed acrylic cockpit keeps you essentially dry — operator's wording is "small bit of spray or damp feet". No wet-weather gear needed, just a streamlined life jacket over normal clothes. Loose comfortable clothing (loose trousers/shorts) is recommended; cockpit can get warm in the sun. Footwear may need to come off per pilot's request. No loose items in pockets — secure storage provided at the kiosk.
Contraindications & motion sickness Not recommended for pregnant riders or anyone with a serious heart condition. Operator explicitly compares the g-forces to a stunt plane — if you're prone to motion sickness, tell the pilot at check-in and they'll dial the trip back to mild. Tight 1-person cockpit; if anyone in the group is claustrophobic, mention at booking. The full height (≤195cm) and weight (≤105kg) limits are for safe emergency egress.
Weather caveat Hydro Attack is more weather-sensitive than river jet boats — Lake Wakatipu gets choppy on windy days. Call 0508 HYDRO ATTACK at least 1 hour before your slot to confirm. If cancelled by weather, they typically reschedule or offer a full refund.
PART 03 · AFTERNOON

The drive to Paradise

15:00
15 min
FUEL

BP Queenstown Truckstop — high-flow diesel

131 Gorge Rd, Queenstown · 24/7 · truckstop pricing · Google Map ↗

4 minutes from Hampshire. The only true truckstop in Queenstown — high-flow diesel nozzles, drive-through bays sized for tour buses, and prices typically 15–25¢/L cheaper than the BP Connect or Z on Frankton Rd. There's no fuel between Queenstown and Glenorchy, and you don't want to drive that road on a quarter tank. Top up to full (~40 L for the Maui River — see fuel panel below).

Why here, not on Frankton Road BP Connect Queenstown (1094 Frankton Rd) and Z Queenstown are both forecourt-only, standard nozzles, retail pricing — fine for cars, slow for a Maui's 95-litre tank. Gorge Rd Truckstop fills it in under 4 minutes.
15:15
25 min · 18 km
DRIVE

Queenstown → first lookouts — Glenorchy-Queenstown Road

SH6A through town, then Glenorchy-Queenstown Rd · Route ↗

The road is fully sealed and motorhome-friendly the whole way to Glenorchy — but it's windy with steep drop-offs and sharp corners. Easy 80 km/h pace, no hurry. The first informal pull-out is at Little Thailand Viewpoint (4 km from town, gravel bay on the right) — a quick 5 min stop for the first big lake view, but skip if it's busy; better viewpoints come.

Time bonus Starting the Glenorchy run at 15:15 (rather than 15:45 with a Shotover-Jet plan) means a full hour at Bob's Cove instead of a rushed half-loop, and Bennetts Bluff in stronger 16:00 light. Use the buffer; don't compress the stops.
15:40
60 min
SHORT WALK

Bob's Cove Track — forest, jetty, lookout

Glenorchy-Queenstown Rd, 17 km from Queenstown · large gravel car park · Google Map ↗

A 1.5 km loop through beech forest opens to a turquoise cove, then a short steep climb to a lookout knoll. Roughly 30 min walking + time at the jetty and lookout — the full hour means an unhurried pace, photos at the cove, and the climb to the viewpoint without rushing. This is one of the best short walks in the whole Wakatipu basin — the sheltered cove looks tropical on a calm afternoon (lake's still glacier-cold; nobody's swimming in November). Toilets at the car park, easy motorhome parking.

16:45
30 min
LOOKOUT

Bennetts Bluff Lookout — the premier viewpoint

Glenorchy-Queenstown Rd, ~25 km from Queenstown · purpose-built 2021 lookout · Google Map ↗

This is THE photo of the Glenorchy drive. Two hundred metres past the old roadside pull-out, a proper purpose-built car park (large enough for the Maui), a 3-minute walk to the platform, picnic tables, toilets. Views open south over the Wakatipu narrows to Pigeon and Pig Islands, and north up the lake to the Humboldts and Mt Earnslaw. Late afternoon sun (around 16:45–17:15) lights the western flank of the mountains and turns the lake aluminium-blue.

Best photo spot Walk past the main platform another 30 m along the path for the view of the road itself snaking down to the lake — the "iconic" Glenorchy Road shot.

DRIVE

Bennetts Bluff → Glenorchy

Continue NW on Glenorchy-Queenstown Rd · Route ↗

The lake widens, the head of Wakatipu opens up, and braided rivers start braiding. Pass Pigeon Island, then over the Rees River flats. Glenorchy (pop. ~365) is one building thick — a general store, two cafés, a tiny museum, and the iconic red boat shed on the wharf.

17:40
75 min
PHOTOS + WALK

Glenorchy Wharf, Red Shed & Walkway — golden hour

Mull St / Oban St · free public car park at the wharf · Google Map ↗

Park at the lakefront. The famous red boat shed sits on the jetty — once a freight depot for the TSS Earnslaw, now the most photographed shed in the South Island. The Humboldt Mountains rise behind it; in afternoon light (~17:45 in mid-November) the peaks are still in sun while the shed sits in shadow — perfect for a silhouette shot.

Then the Glenorchy Walkway — a flat, easy 1-hour loop on a boardwalk through the lagoon wetland behind town. Bird life (paradise ducks, scaup, the occasional fernbird), 360° mountain views, almost no tourists. The Mt Earnslaw glacier glows pink at sunset. This is the moment you remember the trip for. With the time recovered from skipping the Arthurs Point round-trip, you can do the full walkway loop AND have a slow coffee on the waterfront before dinner.

Lord of the Rings note If you're a fan: the flats north of Glenorchy (toward Paradise) appeared as Lothlórien, Isengard, and the muster of Rohan. Paradise itself is 20 km further on a partly-gravel road — too far for today, but the walkway crosses some of the same vista.
18:55
60 min
EARLY DINNER

Dinner at Mrs Woolly's General Store or Glenorchy Café

47 Oban St / Mull St · Mrs Woolly's ↗ · Glenorchy Café ↗

Two solid choices, both walking distance from the wharf: Mrs Woolly's — beautifully restored, woodfired pizzas (NZ$22–28), curated NZ wines and craft beer, fire roaring inside. Closes 19:00 sharp in shoulder season, so order by 18:30. Glenorchy Café — homier, more pies/burgers/lamb, open later until 20:00 most nights.

Alternative: dinner back in Queenstown If the group prefers a Queenstown dinner with more options, skip Glenorchy dinner, grab coffees + slices at the café, and aim back into Queenstown for 20:30 dinner downtown. Fergburger, Public Kitchen, or a quick Asian eats run on Beach St.

The Maui River 6-berth on a 5.0L Mercedes Sprinter chassis burns roughly 13–17 L/100km depending on hills and load — the Glenorchy road has gentle gradients, so today comes in around 15 L/100km. Distance breakdown: Hampshire → Arrowtown (22 km) → Frankton (22 km) → Queenstown lakefront for Hydro Attack (~5 km) → Gorge Rd Truckstop (~3 km) → Glenorchy (47 km) → Hampshire (47 km). Total ~138 km, ~21 L for the day.

Recommended refuel: BP Queenstown Truckstop, 131 Gorge Road — Queenstown's only true high-flow diesel truckstop, open 24/7, with drive-through bays and truckstop pricing (typically 15–25¢/L below the Frankton Road BP Connect and Z stations). It sits 4 minutes from Hampshire and 6 minutes from the Glenorchy road exit, so it slots in perfectly between the morning Arrowtown loop and the afternoon Glenorchy drive. There is no fuel between Queenstown and Glenorchy — top up to full before heading west.

Backup options if Gorge Rd is closed: BP Connect Queenstown (1094 Frankton Rd) and NPD Frankton both have diesel — forecourt-only, retail pricing, but reliable. NPD usually undercuts BP/Z by a few cents.
