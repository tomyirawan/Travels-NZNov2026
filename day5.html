Why Arrowtown morning, Glenorchy afternoon?

The day is built around two natural rhythms: a quiet old town, and a long lakeshore drive. Both have a "right" time of day, and they sit on opposite sides of Queenstown — so the loop is clean if you sequence them correctly.

Light & crowds favour morning

Buckingham Street's sycamores and miners' cottages face east-southeast — soft, low light hits the verandahs before 10:30. The first Queenstown coach tours arrive 10:30–11:30 and motorhome parking on Ramshaw Lane tightens fast. Arrive 09:00, leave by 11:30.

Glenorchy Road is a westbound drive

From Queenstown, the road curves northwest along the lake's western shore. Going out in the afternoon puts the sun behind your right shoulder — every lookout (especially Bennetts Bluff) photographs cleanly, and the red Glenorchy boat shed glows against the Humboldts from 16:00 onward.

The loop has only one unavoidable backtrack

Hampshire → Arrowtown → Frankton (Buzzstop, Shotover Jet, fuel) → Glenorchy → Hampshire. There is no road around Lake Wakatipu's head — the return from Glenorchy retraces the same highway, but in evening light it's a different drive entirely.

The main Buckingham Street parking is for cars only. Ramshaw Lane is two minutes' walk from the heritage street and the only sensible motorhome park in town. Free, no time limit, public toilets on-site. Get here before 09:30 — by 10:30 the coach tours arrive and the long bays go first.

Heads-up Do NOT try to drive a 7m motorhome down Buckingham St itself. It's one-way, narrow, angled-parking on both sides. Park at Ramshaw and walk in.

WANDER

Buckingham Street — heritage stroll & coffee

Buckingham St, 1860s miners' cottages, 24 trees each side · Google Map ↗

Walk east down Buckingham toward the Arrow River. Sixty-plus original gold-rush buildings still standing, many now boutique shops, galleries, and cafés. The sycamores arching over the street are spectacular in spring green by mid-November. Don't miss the Mary Cotter sycamore outside the old Cotter cottage — there's a small plaque dedicated to a two-year-old in 1867.

Coffee + breakfast at Provisions of Arrowtown (34 Buckingham St) — set in a historic miner's cottage with a back garden. The Eggs Benedict and the pastries are both legendary; NZ$18–32 for a main. Or a quicker stop at The Chop Shop (Arrow Lane) — locals' favourite for flat whites.

Sweet stop for the group The Remarkable Sweet Shop and Patagonia Chocolates are both on Buckingham — pick up roadtrip snacks for the Glenorchy drive.

HISTORY

Arrowtown Chinese Settlement — free, riverside

Bush Creek end of Buckingham St · Google Map ↗

A two-minute walk past the western end of Buckingham, beside Bush Creek, you'll find a cluster of restored stone-and-tin huts. From 1869, Chinese gold-miners — invited by the government when European miners moved west to the Coast — were forced to live separately here. Ah Lum's Store is the standout: fully restored 1880s provisions shop with original counters and ledgers. Information panels along the path, free entry, allow 25–30 minutes.

HONEY

The Buzz Stop Honey Centre & Café — Country Lane, Frankton

7 Hansen Rd, Frankton · Country Lane Retail Village · 7 days, cafe 8:30–3pm · Google Map ↗

Nick Cameron's family has been beekeeping in Central Otago for over 100 years. The centre is part shop, part observation hive (thousands of bees behind glass), part café with views of the Remarkables. Taste the full Active Manuka range from 5+ to 24+, plus native honeys you won't find at the Cromwell honey shed — rātā, kāmahi, southern thyme. Pick up jars for home and the famously good honey-glazed liege waffles in the café.

If you've got energy for one more activity, the Spin Your Own Honey hands-on tour runs at 10:00 and 15:00 — too tight for today's plan. Buy a jar instead and keep moving toward lunch and the canyon.

Why the second honey stop is worth it The Cromwell honesty-box shed yesterday was farm-direct single-source; Buzzstop is a curated NZ-wide tasting bench. Different products, different role. Some travellers stock the van here for the rest of the road trip.

OPTIONAL · ADRENALINE

Shotover Jet — Arthurs Point

Shotover Jet Beach Rd, Arthurs Point · adult NZ$179, child (5–15) NZ$99 · Google Map ↗

25 minutes on the boat, 90 minutes start-to-finish. Operating hourly 10:00–16:00, 7 days. The world's oldest commercial jet boat (since 1965), and the only operator permitted in the dramatic narrow Shotover River canyons — 85 km/h between sheer schist walls, signature 360° spins on the wide stretches. Free shuttle from the Station Building (Cnr Camp & Shotover Sts, Queenstown) — check in 45 minutes before your trip time. Or self-drive to the Arthurs Point base (10 min from town, free parking, complimentary lockers) and check in 30 minutes prior.

What to wear, how wet you get You will catch some spray during the 360° spins, especially on outer seats — operator provides a full-length waterproof coat under your life jacket, so jeans and tops stay mostly dry, but hair and cuffs do not. Centre seats are driest. Bring a beanie or hood: wind-chill at 85 km/h in the canyon shade is real. Heated handrails on the boats. Lockers at the base for valuables.
Why this time slot A 13:30 or 14:00 departure leaves you back on the road by 15:00 — enough light to drive to Glenorchy, reach Bennetts Bluff before 16:00, and still make the Glenorchy boat shed golden hour. Any later and you'll be racing the light.
Contraindications Not recommended for pregnant riders or anyone with serious back/neck issues. Minimum height 1.0m (can be raised to 1.2m if river is running high). Hands free at all times — GoPros must be head-mounted, no handheld phones.

FUEL

BP Queenstown Truckstop — high-flow diesel

131 Gorge Rd, Queenstown · 24/7 · truckstop pricing · Google Map ↗

On the way back from Arthurs Point, Gorge Road takes you straight past the BP truckstop — the only true high-flow diesel station in Queenstown. Drive-through bays sized for tour buses, prices typically 15–25¢/L cheaper than Frankton Rd forecourts. There's no fuel between Queenstown and Glenorchy, and you don't want to drive that road on a quarter tank. Top up to full (~40 L for the Maui River — see fuel panel below).

Why here, not on Frankton Road BP Connect Queenstown (1094 Frankton Rd) and Z Queenstown are both forecourt-only, standard nozzles, retail pricing — fine for cars, slow for a Maui's 95-litre tank. Gorge Rd Truckstop fills it in under 4 minutes and is geographically on-route between Shotover Jet at Arthurs Point and the Glenorchy turn-off.

SHORT WALK

Bob's Cove Track — forest, jetty, lookout

Glenorchy-Queenstown Rd, 17 km from Queenstown · large gravel car park · Google Map ↗

A 1.5 km loop through beech forest opens to a turquoise cove, then a short steep climb to a lookout knoll. Roughly 30 min walking + 10–15 min at the jetty + lookout. This is one of the best short walks in the whole Wakatipu basin — the sheltered cove looks tropical on a calm afternoon (lake's still glacier-cold; nobody's swimming in November). Toilets at the car park, easy motorhome parking.

Skip-it call If you're behind schedule after Shotover Jet, skip Bob's Cove — Bennetts Bluff (next stop) is non-negotiable and you don't want to rush it.

LOOKOUT

Bennetts Bluff Lookout — the premier viewpoint

Glenorchy-Queenstown Rd, ~25 km from Queenstown · purpose-built 2021 lookout · Google Map ↗

This is THE photo of the Glenorchy drive. Two hundred metres past the old roadside pull-out, a proper purpose-built car park (large enough for the Maui), a 3-minute walk to the platform, picnic tables, toilets. Views open south over the Wakatipu narrows to Pigeon and Pig Islands, and north up the lake to the Humboldts and Mt Earnslaw. Late afternoon sun (around 17:00) lights the western flank of the mountains and turns the lake aluminium-blue.

Best photo spot Walk past the main platform another 30 m along the path for the view of the road itself snaking down to the lake — the "iconic" Glenorchy Road shot.

PHOTOS + WALK

Glenorchy Wharf, Red Shed & Walkway — golden hour

Mull St / Oban St · free public car park at the wharf · Google Map ↗

Park at the lakefront. The famous red boat shed sits on the jetty — once a freight depot for the TSS Earnslaw, now the most photographed shed in the South Island. The Humboldt Mountains rise behind it; in afternoon light (~18:00 in mid-November) the peaks are still in sun while the shed sits in shadow — perfect for a silhouette shot.

Then the Glenorchy Walkway — a flat, easy 1-hour loop on a boardwalk through the lagoon wetland behind town. Bird life (paradise ducks, scaup, the occasional fernbird), 360° mountain views, almost no tourists. The Mt Earnslaw glacier glows pink at sunset. This is the moment you remember the trip for.

Lord of the Rings note If you're a fan: the flats north of Glenorchy (toward Paradise) appeared as Lothlórien, Isengard, and the muster of Rohan. Paradise itself is 20 km further on a partly-gravel road — too far for today, but the walkway crosses some of the same vista.

The Maui River 6-berth on a 5.0L Mercedes Sprinter chassis burns roughly 13–17 L/100km depending on hills and load — the Glenorchy road has gentle gradients, so today comes in around 15 L/100km. Distance breakdown: Hampshire → Arrowtown (22 km) → Frankton (22 km) → Shotover Jet base at Arthurs Point (~5 km out-and-back) → Gorge Rd Truckstop (~3 km) → Glenorchy (47 km) → Hampshire (47 km). Total ~146 km, ~22 L for the day.

Recommended refuel: BP Queenstown Truckstop, 131 Gorge Road — Queenstown's only true high-flow diesel truckstop, open 24/7, with drive-through bays and truckstop pricing (typically 15–25¢/L below the Frankton Road BP Connect and Z stations). It sits 4 minutes from Hampshire and 6 minutes from the Glenorchy road exit, so it slots in perfectly between the morning Arrowtown loop and the afternoon Glenorchy drive. There is no fuel between Queenstown and Glenorchy — top up to full before heading west.

Backup options if Gorge Rd is closed: BP Connect Queenstown (1094 Frankton Rd) and NPD Frankton both have diesel — forecourt-only, retail pricing, but reliable. NPD usually undercuts BP/Z by a few cents.
