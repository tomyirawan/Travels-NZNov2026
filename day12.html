Route · Coastal SH1

Down the gorge to Christchurch's edge, then north along the rebuilt coast.

Why coastal and why these stops.

SH73 down the Waimakariri gorge, skirt the northwest edge of Christchurch via the bypass, then north on SH1 — the rebuilt earthquake coast. This is the route most travellers actually use, and for good reason: it's the fastest with the most concentrated scenery in the back half.

The first 2 hours after Castle Hill are workmanlike — getting yourself to the coast. The reward starts at Cheviot: first hills, first sea-views, and a genuinely good cafe lunch. From there, the next 2 hours are postcard country — fur seals, sweeping bay views, snow-capped ranges falling into the Pacific.

"The 2016 earthquake raised this coastline by up to a metre. The road you'll drive on north of Oaro is largely rebuilt — seawalls, slip mitigation, the works. It's a quiet engineering marvel."

Verify before you leave: Check the NZTA Journey Planner on the morning of 18 Nov for SH1 status. The coast is generally open in 2026 but still occasionally closes for slips or weather. If it's closed, divert at Waiau onto Inland Route 70 — adds about 1 hour. [verify day-of]
Morning Highlight

Kura Tāwhiti — the treasure of the distant places.

The early start pays for itself, right here.

You're starting at 8 AM. By 8:15 you're on the loop. The first tour buses don't roll in until around 10:30. Between 8 and 9:30, Castle Hill is almost yours.

The morning light hits the eastern faces of the tors at a low angle — this is golden hour for the limestone. The 9 AM crowd misses it.

The walk: 1.5 km loop, mostly easy with a short climb. 60–90 min lets you wander, scramble, and photograph. Solid shoes — some surfaces are dewy and slick before the sun hits them.

Wind warning: Plateau at 700 m. Even a sunny November morning can be 10–12°C with bite. Layer up — windproof shell is non-optional.

MUST · Wildlife

Point Kean Seal Colony

End of Fyffe Quay, Kaikōura Peninsula · Free parking · Toilets

New Zealand fur seals (kekeno) sprawl on the rocks at the tip of the peninsula. Late afternoon is the sweet spot — seals are active, light is warm, tour groups have departed for the day.

Distance: stay 10 m back minimum. Seals look sleepy but they move fast and bite hard, especially mothers with pups (November is start of pupping season). Treat them like grizzly bears with flippers.

If you have energy: continue along the Kaikōura Peninsula Walkway (clifftop loop, ~3 hours full circuit, but the first 30 min gives you the headland view). Otherwise the Point Kean carpark alone delivers the wildlife encounter without the hike.

Afternoon Highlight

The colony at Point Kean.

What you'll actually see at Point Kean.

Several dozen New Zealand fur seals (Arctocephalus forsteri, kekeno) at any given time. Mostly males in November — females and pups gather in larger numbers later in summer.

They sleep on rocks for hours, then suddenly trundle into the surf for a fishing trip. They are wild, fast over short distances, and have a serious bite. The 10m-distance rule isn't arbitrary.

"The 2016 earthquake raised the Kaikōura coast by up to a metre. New seal hauling-out platforms appeared overnight. The colony quietly expanded."

About Ohau Stream Walkway: The famous seal-pup waterfall north of Kaikōura has been permanently closed since 2016 earthquake damage. Some visitors enter at their own risk; this itinerary does not. Point Kean is the safe, legal, and equally rewarding alternative. [high confidence]

If Things Change

Backup plans · contingencies · weather.

If SH1 is closed south of Kaikōura — Take Inland Road (Route 70) from Waiau via Mt Lyford. Slower (parts at 30 km/h), narrower, scenic alpine country. Add 1 hour minimum. Confirm open at NZTA before committing.
If weather closes in at Castle Hill — The tors lose their magic in heavy rain or low cloud. Visit Cave Stream upper viewpoint instead and leave early. Use the gained time for an extended seal-colony stop or for Kaikōura Peninsula Walkway.
If you're behind schedule by Cheviot — Skip Gore Bay Lookout (Cathedral Cliffs). It's beautiful but optional. Push straight through to Kaikōura.
If you want the crayfish experience — Nin's Bin (SH1, 30 km north of Kaikōura) is open Tue 10am–4pm. But it's an hour of doubling back from your Top 10 stay. Only do this if crayfish is a bucket-list item. Otherwise: Cooper's Catch in town has half-crayfish for less hassle.
Seal pupping season caveat — November is start of pupping season. Mothers can be aggressive. 10m minimum distance is non-negotiable. [medium confidence on aggression timing]
